Meditation Centre Development

We have relocated part of our public programs and suspended others in due respect to an order given by The City of Edmonton to cease public services offered at our current facility due to bylaw restrictions.

Our goal is to purchase or build a facility in a location where we have the permission for Religious Assembly so that we can resume our meditation programs, Dhamma discussions, children's programs, youth programs, mindfulness development programs, stress relief programs, counselling, retreats and daily sermons, etc. that we offered, without any hindrance. We also want to ensure that there is sufficient space available for hosting our annual religious, cultural and community programs in it.

Purchase of a this kind of facility is much more financially challenging than buying a residential place. However, we are confident that this challenge can be overcome, and allow this meditation centre to continue its public service, thanking to the commitment and generosity of the local and global community.

Financial Status Overview

In current market conditions, it requires about a minimum investment of 1.5 million dollars to purchase or build a suitable facility for the meditation centre in a location where there is permission for Religious Assembly. Towards this, we can allocate about $500,000 by selling the current house where the meditation centre is established at, and also obtain loan of about $300,000 from the bank. This leaves a shortfall of about $700,000.

To cover this shortfall, we have established a Meditation Centre Development Fund and started obtaining pledges from our devotees and well wishers. Contributions made to this fund will solely be used for the establishment of the new meditation centre facility. We kindly request the generous people in Edmonton, across Canada and the World, to come forward and assist us in this difficult but noble mission.
